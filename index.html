FUTURE CIVILIZATIONS

An Inter-Ivy initiative led by Cornell University exploring how artificial intelligence and autonomous systems will redefine the structures that sustain human life—through the lens of the “Next Human City".

April 2026 . Cornell University

Four Thematic Pillars

The program is organized around four pillars exploring how emerging technologies will reshape cities, society, food systems, and even civilization beyond Earth.

Designing the Autonomous City

How AI, robotics, and autonomous systems will transform architecture, mobility, infrastructure, and the experience of public space.

Human Psychology, Purpose & Social Life

Identity, belonging, creativity, and emotional life in an AI-shaped world— from autonomous agents to automated social systems.

Sustainability & Agriculture

AI-driven agriculture, resource distribution, food systems, and environmental technologies that sustain future civilizations.

Frontier Cities

A serious exploration of off-world settlements—how civilization could extend to Mars and how AI may be essential to building new forms of cities.

Live Demonstrations

Interactive exhibitions highlighting research and creative work at the boundary of AI, robotics, design, and society. Each demonstration links to the lab or group behind it.

Fashion Robotics — Maia Hirsch

Wearable technology, soft robotics, and fashion—garments that respond to the body, environment, and social interaction, presented as interactive robotic dresses and “living systems.”

Visit Lab

Automation & Robotics Laboratory (AR Lab) — Lirong Xiang

AI-driven robotics for sustainable agriculture, integrating computer vision and machine learning. Featuring Spyder Bot, a legged agricultural robot for autonomous sensing and monitoring.

Visit Lab

Machine Poetics — Sangh Leigh

Art, design, and robotics exploring embodied human–machine relationships. Featuring A Bit of the Stranger, an experience reflecting on intimacy and coexistence with AI.
