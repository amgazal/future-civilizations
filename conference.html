About

What the conference is about

The Future Civilizations Conference is an Inter-Ivy initiative led by Cornell University, convening scholars, engineers, architects, artists, designers, and builders to examine how artificial intelligence and autonomous systems will redefine the structures that sustain human life. The conference is framed around one central question: what does the next human city look like?

Themes

The four thematic pillars

A program built to feel both rigorous and alive — ideas you can debate, and systems you can actually see.

Designing the Autonomous City

How AI, robotics, and autonomous systems will transform the built environment, including architecture, mobility, urban planning, infrastructure, and public space.

Human Psychology & Social Life

How identity, belonging, creativity, and emotional life may evolve when everyday experience is deeply integrated with autonomous agents and automated systems.

Sustainability & Future Agriculture

How AI-driven agriculture, food systems, environmental technologies, and resource distribution will sustain future cities and human populations.

Frontier Cities Beyond Earth

A speculative but serious exploration of how these principles extend to off-world settlements such as Mars, where AI may become essential to survival and design.
