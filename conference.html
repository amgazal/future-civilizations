Future Civilizations Conference

April 18, 2026 • Cornell University

Conference Vision

The Future Civilizations Conference is an Inter-Ivy initiative led by Cornell University, convening scholars, engineers, architects, artists, designers, and builders to explore how artificial intelligence and autonomous systems will redefine the structures that sustain human life. The conference is framed around a core theme: the “Next Human City.”

Rather than treating the future as speculation, the conference creates space for interdisciplinary dialogue that connects technical innovation with human values, ethical responsibility, and long-term societal planning.

What Will Happen at the Conference

Speaker Addresses

Speakers from academia and industry will deliver talks exploring the technological, social, and ethical dimensions of AI-driven futures—offering both big-picture vision and current research.

Panel Conversations

Moderated panels bring together different disciplines to debate real-world challenges and future questions—designed to surface tensions, tradeoffs, and new ideas across fields.

Audience Q&A

Attendees will have dedicated opportunities to ask questions and engage directly with speakers and panelists—testing ideas, challenging assumptions, and exploring unanswered questions.

Live Demonstrations

Interactive exhibitions will showcase real applications of robotics, AI, design, and human–machine interaction—turning abstract concepts into tangible experiences.

Four Thematic Pillars

Designing the Autonomous City

How AI, robotics, and autonomous systems will transform architecture, mobility, infrastructure, and the experience of public space.

Human Psychology, Purpose, and Social Life

Identity, belonging, creativity, and emotional life in an AI-shaped world—ranging from autonomous agents to automated social systems and new forms of community.

Sustainability & Agriculture

AI-driven agriculture, resource distribution, and environmental systems that sustain future civilizations—especially under climate pressure and limited resources.

Frontier Cities

A serious exploration of off-world settlements—how civilization could extend to Mars and how AI may be essential to building new forms of cities beyond Earth.

Live Demonstrations

These exhibitions highlight research and creative work at the boundary of AI, robotics, design, and society. Each demonstration includes a link to the lab or group behind it.

Fashion Robotics — Maia Hirsch

Wearable technology and soft robotics garments that respond to the body, environment, and social interaction—presented as interactive robotic dresses and “living systems.”
