Conference Vision

The Future Civilizations Conference is an Inter-Ivy initiative led by Cornell University, convening scholars, engineers, architects, artists, designers, and builders to explore how artificial intelligence and autonomous systems will redefine the structures that sustain human life. The conference is framed around a core theme: the “Next Human City.”.


The Four Thematic Pillars

A program built to feel both rigorous and alive — ideas you can argue with, and systems you can see. With each pillar, we help you explore and identify your questions and hope to have answers for such questions through our conference.

Designing the Autonomous City

How AI, robotics, and autonomous systems will transform the built environment, including architecture, mobility, urban planning, infrastructure, and the experience of public space.

Human Psychology & Social Life

How human identity, belonging, creativity, and emotional life might evolve when everyday life is deeply integrated with autonomous agents, from caregiving robots to AI therapists to automated social systems.

Sustainability & Future Agriculture

How AI-driven agriculture, resource distribution, food systems, and environmental technologies will sustain future cities and human populations

Frontier Cities Beyond Earth

A speculative but serious exploration of how these principles extend to off-world environments such as Mars and how AI will be essential to building entirely new forms of settlements.



Live Demos

These exhibitions highlight research and creative work at the boundary of AI, robotics, design, and society. Each demonstration includes a link to the lab or group behind it.

Fashion Robotics Demo

Interactive robotic dresses — wearable systems that act like living interfaces.

AR Lab: Spyder Bot

Autonomous sensing for food systems: field perception, monitoring, and decision-making.

Machine Poetics

Human-machine expression — what “creativity” becomes when systems co-author meaning.
